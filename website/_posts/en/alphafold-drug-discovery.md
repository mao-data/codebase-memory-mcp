## AlphaFold Changed Everything—Then Evolved

In 2020, DeepMind's AlphaFold 2 solved one of biology's most important 50-year-old problems: protein structure prediction. It let scientists understand the 3D shape of proteins at unprecedented speed—and protein shape determines whether a drug molecule can bind to it and work.

But AlphaFold was just the beginning. In February 2026, Isomorphic Labs—DeepMind's drug discovery spinoff led by Demis Hassabis—released a system that stunned the industry: **IsoDDE**.

---

## IsoDDE: Doubling AlphaFold 3's Accuracy

IsoDDE dramatically outperforms its predecessor on key metrics:

- **Doubles AlphaFold 3's accuracy on novel systems**
- **Outperforms Boltz-2 by nearly 20× on antibody predictions**
- **Beats physics-based methods on binding affinity at a fraction of the cost and time**

This isn't just academic achievement. More accurate protein-drug binding prediction means fewer failed experiments, faster candidate screening, and higher clinical success rates. Traditional drug development averages 10–15 years and over $2.6 billion per approved drug—AI is systematically compressing both numbers.

---

## Zovegalisib: An AI-Designed Cancer Drug in Phase 3

Isomorphic's lead drug, **Zovegalisib**, is a first-in-class PI3Ka inhibitor designed to selectively target cancer-driving mutations while sparing the normal protein version.

Current status:
- Received **FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation**
- Entered **Phase 3 trials for metastatic breast cancer**
- Hassabis says more AI-designed drugs will enter **Phase 1 clinical trials by end of 2026**

FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation is a meaningful signal: it indicates regulatory recognition that the drug has potential to substantially improve on existing treatments and triggers priority review.

## Other Notable AI Drug Discovery Cases

**Absci's ABS-201**: Developed entirely through "zero-shot AI antibody design" with no traditional screening process. This antibody targeting hair loss and endometriosis is currently in Phase 1/2a trials.

**Insilico Medicine**: Its AI-designed drug INS018_055 for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis has entered Phase 2—the world's first fully AI-designed small molecule in Phase 2 trials.

---

## The Three Pillars of AI Drug Discovery

**1. Target identification**: AI analyzes vast genomic datasets to identify the most promising disease-driving protein targets. What once required years of literature review now takes weeks.

**2. Molecular design**: AlphaFold/IsoDDE-class systems predict the 3D structure and binding capability of candidate molecules, making "virtual screening" a viable replacement for some lab-based screening.

**3. Optimization and prediction**: AI predicts ADMET properties (absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion, toxicity), filtering out the most likely-to-fail candidates before animal studies begin.

---

## The Honest Caveat: AI Can't Replace Everything

The highest failure rate in drug development still occurs in Phase 2/3 clinical trials—because clinical efficacy depends not just on molecular binding but on immune responses, individual variation, and long-term safety, all of which AI still struggles to fully model.

AI has compressed the "first half" of drug discovery. It cannot eliminate clinical trial uncertainty.

---

## Conclusion: The Starting Line of a New Era

In 2026, the first drugs designed "from discovery to clinical trial by AI" are entering the human body for the first time in medical history. Whatever Zovegalisib's final outcome, it has already marked a milestone: AI is no longer just an analysis tool. It has become a genuine drug designer.
